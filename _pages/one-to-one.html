One-to-One

As the lead technical partner for the Smart Cities Community Support Program, OpenNorth is launching a One-to-One Advisory Service for Canadian communities participating in the Smart Cities Challenge. The initial phase of the advisory service will roll out in Winter-Spring 2019, focusing on Smart Cities Challenge finalist and applicant communities. There is no cost to participating in the program.

The Offer

OpenNorth’s One-to-One Advisory Service will help you identify and apply best practices tailored to your community needs. By enrolling right now, you will have access to two learning path levels (non-expert, expert) on the following open smart cities spotlight domains:

  1. Citizen engagement
  2. Open procurement
  3. Data and open standards
  4. Governance

This learning path will comprise of online webinars, template creation and personalization, and personalized support. Our service will be offered on a rolling two-month cycle. Enrolment takes place the month before the cycle begins. The next two-month cycle is scheduled for April-May 2019.

Process

In order to participate in a cycle, each finalist would complete the following steps:

  1. Opt-in to the Program
  2. Send us your final proposal
  3. Complete a one-hour organizational self-assessment questionnaire

The One-to-One Advisory Service is designed as a structured process that consists of a tailored needs assessment, capacity-building action plan, and service delivery.

About the Community Solutions Network

In fall 2018, the Government of Canada announced that Evergreen, working in partnership with OpenNorth, their Future Cities Canada collaborative platform and numerous non-profit institutions, foundations and stakeholders across Canada, received funding to deliver activities from the new Smart Cities Community Support Program. The funding supports advisory and capacity-building services for communities nation-wide as they explore and implement smart cities projects. As the program lead, Evergreen is working with OpenNorth, the lead technical partner, and partners to support current finalists and future applicants of the Smart Cities Challenge in advancing their thinking, developing strong final proposals, and strengthening successful implementation of winning projects.

The Community Solutions Network is a community-centric platform, serving every type of Canadian community: big, mid-sized, Indigenous, small and northern. The program will provide useful information, learning opportunities and advisory services for municipal and community leaders, helping to improve the lives of residents.

The Network is for communities of all sizes and in every region - big, mid-sized, Indigenous, small and remote – and provides information, learning opportunities and advisory services for municipal and community leaders to improve the lives of residents.

The program runs parallel to the Government of Canada’s Smart Cities Challenge to amplify its impact and objectives to create more liveable, inclusive communities.
