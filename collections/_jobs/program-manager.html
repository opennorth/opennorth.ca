About Open North

Founded in 2011, Open North is Canada’s leading not-for-profit organization specialized in open data and open government, community engagement, and open smart cities. Our mission is to drive research, capacity-building and network collaboration across and within sectors to advance the responsible and effective use of data & technology in order to achieve our vision of data and technology that empower transparent, accountable and inclusive communities. With a multidisciplinary team of 15 staff (+ expert advisors) and an annual budget of $1M+, we work with the most innovative and connected communities in Canada and internationally. Through our service offering, we are called upon to support our partners develop and implement data governance models, lead collaborative data sharing initiatives, develop strategic plans and operational roadmaps, and train communities on a range of open smart city topics (eg. data management, open procurement, digital inclusion, and ethical AI). As an established international leader, our research and practitioners networks extend across the country and globally.

The Program Manager will have a well-developed understanding of the technical considerations and social impact of data and tech and be able to bring both strong project management expertise and fresh research and policy ideas to the organization.
The Program Manager will have a successful track record of developing and executing research and policy initiatives that align with organizational strengths and values. The candidate will have experience delivering impactful projects within agreed budget, specifications and timelines, as well as managing teams of highly-skilled employees and relationships with clients.
The Program Manager will work closely with and report to the senior management team. The Program Manager will oversee the execution of all key research projects and programs, and supervise and coordinate a team of interdisciplinary researchers and policy experts.
The Program Manager is expected to be located in Montreal. Some travel is required in Canada and could also be expected internationally.

Responsibilities include but are not limited to:

  • Manage and monitor programs strategic implementation
  • Lead strategic meetings and decision processes
  • Oversee programs financial budgets, minimize overrun and manage programs burn rate, negotiate and ensure contract compliance as pertaining to program budget and company policies
  • Monitor, analyze and communicate programs risks and opportunities
  • Build and manage a team of 4-7 mid-level and junior staff, in addition to programs’ expert advisors
  • Lead the recruitment and integration process of new research staff members
  • Negotiate and manage relationships with clients, partners and other stakeholders
  • Prepare and facilitate high-level work meetings with program stakeholders, including funders and national partners
  • Maintain and strengthen high-quality research and service delivery standards
  • Supervise tracking and delivery of program milestones and KPIs for program-wide reporting
  • Track data and technological and innovative trends

The successful candidate must have the following skills and experience:

  • Program management experience of 8+ years (B2G sector a strong asset)
  • Strong knowledge or applied experience related to data & tech (data governance, digital infrastructure, open data, data standards, AI ethics, etc.) and innovation (agile, prototyping, design thinking)
  • Management experience of teams of 5+ people (experience with multidisciplinary & highly-skilled teams an asset)
  • Perfectly bilingual in French & English (mandatory requirement for the position)
  • Ability to communicate effectively to various audience (both verbally and in writing)
  • Multi-stakeholder relations management experience
  • Graduate degree in a relevant field required. MBA or advanced business analytics degree will be considered an asset

The most competitive candidates will also demonstrate one or more of the following skills:

  • Empathic leader, able to motivate and mentor a team
  • Problem-solver, oriented towards results and focused on quality
  • Work both independently and as a strong team player with an aptitude for working in a collaborative environment
  • Exceptional interpersonal, oral and written communication skills
  • Strong political sense and the ability to liaise with clients and assist in the definition and articulation of their needs
  • Creative, innovative, curious and critical thinker
