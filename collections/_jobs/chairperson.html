Mission Statement

To increase government transparency and to inform and empower citizens to exercise their political influence and participate actively in decision-making processes.

Founded in 2011, OpenNorth is Canada’s leading not-for-profit organization specialized in open data, open smart cities, and applied research. We implement a collaborative local-to-global approach to improving government transformation and systems change through a citizen-centered, principles-based, and applied research-led approach to complex multi-stakeholder data-related problems.

Partners throughout the world at local, regional, and national scales benefit from our ability to improve governmental and stakeholder capacity in adopting ethical data practices, managing organizational culture change, and translating data issues to policy contexts. We develop capacity and policies to improve transparency and accountability, increase inclusivity, and build the open smart communities of the future.

About the Board

OpenNorth’s Board supports the work of the organization and provides mission-based leadership and strategic governance. While day-to-day operations are led by OpenNorth’s executive director (ED) and staff, the Board-ED relationship is a partnership, and the appropriate involvement of the Board is both critical and expected.

OpenNorth’s Board members serve one-year terms (to be reviewed in December 2019 by the board). Board meetings are held every three months and committee meetings may be held on an ad hoc basis, in person for those in Montreal, and via video conference for those located elsewhere.

All Board members are expected to take on the following responsibilities:

  1. Ensure the Organization’s Mission and Purpose
  2. Select the Executive Director
  3. Support the Executive and Review His/Her Performance
  4. Ensure Effective Organizational Planning
  5. Ensure Adequate Resources
  6. Manage Resources Effectively
  7. Determine and Monitor the Organization’s Programs and Services
  8. Enhance the Organization’s Public Image

The Chairperson will be responsible for a set of duties; including but not limited to the following:

  • Serves as the chief volunteer of the organization
  • Participates in the search, appointment of the selection committee for hiring the Executive Director
  • Recommends compensation and benefits package of Executive Director to the appropriate committee
  • Is a partner with the Executive Director in achieving the organization’s mission
  • Provides leadership to the Board of Directors, who sets policy and to whom the CEO/Executive Director is accountable
  • Chairs meetings of the board of directors
  • Develops agendas for meetings with the CEO/Executive Director
  • Recommends to the board which committees and task forces are to be established
  • Serves ex officio as a member of committees and attends their meetings when invited
  • Discusses issues confronting the organization with the CEO/Executive Director
  • Helps guide and mediate Board actions with respect to organizational priorities and governance concerns
  • Reviews with the CEO/Executive Director any issues of concern to the board
  • Monitors financial planning and financial reports
  • Evaluates the performance of the CEO/Executive Director and the effectiveness of the board members
  • Annually evaluates the performance of the organization in achieving its mission and objectives
  • Speaks to the media and represents the organization to the community, in coordination with the ED
  • Annually reviews matters of governance that relate to the board’s structure, role, and relationship to management

Qualifications

Ideal candidates for the Chairperson position will possess some, or all, of the following leadership characteristics and interpersonal skills:

  • Relevant local and international professional experience in urban planning, open government, and / or applied research
  • Prior experience with non-profit management is preferred
  • Prior experience in the public sector having worked at municipal, provincial/ territorial or federal government organizations
  • Fundraising, business development, growth management experience
  • A commitment to and understanding of OpenNorth’s unique value proposition, with an appreciation for the technology sector, community building and citizen engagement
  • Savvy diplomatic skills and a natural affinity for cultivating relationships and persuading, convening, facilitating, and building consensus among diverse individuals
  • Ability-to-influence competencies, including connections and influence with key people and using connections to advance the organization’s interests and values
  • Personal qualities of integrity, proactivity, credibility, commitment to inclusion and collaboration
  • A commitment to getting things done, a friendly persona, and an ability to resolve contentious issues calmly and fairly
  • Prior non-profit/charitable Board leadership experience is considered an asset.

Please note: Service on OpenNorth’s Board of Directors is without remuneration, except for administrative support, travel, and accommodation costs in relation to Board members’ duties.
