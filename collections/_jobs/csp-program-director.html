Background

In October 2018, the Government of Canada selected Evergreen and its partners, including OpenNorth, to receive up to $4.6 million in Smart Cities Community Support Program funding over two years, until March 2020. The Community Solutions Network (CSN) was established with this funding as a community-centric platform, providing communities of all sizes with the knowledge, expertise, experience and guidance they need to build internal capacity and navigate the smart cities landscape through the One-to-One Advisory Service, and event-based programs.

OpenNorth is the lead technical partner of the CSN. It is responsible for the network’s One-to-One Advisory Service with a specific approach and focus on open smart cities. The Advisory Service offers 25 basic and advanced courses online, over a dozen set of implementation guidelines, policy templates, as well as high value personalized guidance from a team of 10 expert advisors and organizational partners on a range of open smart cities topics, including open standards in procurement, implementing a privacy policy, approaches to data and tech literacy, developing ethical guidelines on artificial intelligence, and building awareness of indigenous data sovereignty, and much more.

The intended audiences of the Advisory Service are elected officials, high level decision makers, and technical leads within cities and communities. Up to 75 communities will be engaged through the Advisory Service before March 2020. The program has been designed to scale and is part of the CSN’s long term strategy. A wide range of partnerships and business development opportunities have been identified and are actively tracking to build upon the program beyond March 2020.

About Open North

The Advisory Service builds upon OpenNorth’s track record of promoting intra and interjurisdictional data interoperability, policy coherence, and critical insight into data ecosystems. Founded in 2011, OpenNorth is Canada’s leading not-for-profit organization specialized in applied research and open smart cities. We work with Canada’s largest, most innovative, and connected cities. Our network extends across the country and globally through national and global communities of practices and open government partners. Our work promotes the effective use of data as infrastructure facilitating sustained engagement between government, civil society, the public, and other sectors.

Specifically, OpenNorth brings its extensive experience in the development of data standards, open data strategies, and multi-stakeholder problem identification, to develop this Advisory Service with a strong grounding in the practical concerns of Canadian cities and communities. The Advisory Service is further supported by OpenNorth’s network of leading academics and practitioners in Canada and internationally. Through this combination of expertise and experience, OpenNorth is bringing values of transparency, accountability, inclusion, and interoperability, to smart cities in its one of a-kind capacity building Advisory Service.
